1. An Old Man Lived in the Village

As the princess went ice skating the Red panda burst out of the snow and jumped into the princess’s arms. They instantly became the best of friends. On the way home she told Yoda to hop on her back. As soon as she got home to the castle with the red panda, her father, The King, had a horrible look on his face, she could tell he was very cross. The King screeched, “Why do you have a red panda on your back, I HATE red pandas. Get rid of him, now!” “No” cried the princess. “His name is Yoda and he is soft and cuddly and he doesn’t bite at all.” “No” said the king. The princess and Yoda didn’t like his choice. Whenever the King was around Yoda had to hide. One night at midnight the King caught Yoda hiding in the lounge room and grabbed him and put him in the box and put it outside for the rubbish truck. The next morning the princess went to find Yoda but she could not find him anywhere. She searched the whole castle but he wasn’t there so she went outside. She heard a sneeze coming from the rubbish. She went to look and found a small box with crying noises coming from inside. She tried to open the box but she couldn’t so she went back inside to find something to open the box. Her mother reminded her that she promised her father the king that she would stay inside and do her homework. She hides the box in the bushes then goes to do her homework. That night when everyone was fast asleep she went back to get Yoda out of the box. She finally got the box open and carried Yoda to safety. The princess thought maybe she could make him a home in the garden. The next day she started to make Yoda’s home in the garden. The King watched the princess from the castle window. The king felt bad for being mean and decided to help her. Together, they started to make a beautiful tree house for Yoda to live in. The king promised that Yoda could stay as long as he stayed in the tree house. They all lived happily ever after.

2. Having A Best Friend

A story tells that two friends were walking through the desert. During some point of the journey they had an argumentz and one friend slapped the other one in the face.The one who got slapped was hurt but without saying anything wrote in the sandtoday my best friend slapped me in the face.they kept on walking until they found an oasis, where they decided to take a bath. The one who had been slapped got stuck in the mire and started drowning, but the friend saved him. After he recovered from the near drowning, he wrote on a stoneToday my best friend saved my life.The friend who had slapped and saved his best friend asked him; I hurt you, you wrote in the sand and now you write on a ston why The other friend replied When someone hurts us we should write it down in sand where winds of forgiveness can erase it away. But when someone does something good for us, we must engrave it in stone where no wind can ever erase it.Moral of the story: Don t value the things you have in your life. But value who you have in your life.
